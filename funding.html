Tenant Financial Assistance

Explore programs and resources to help with deposits, rent, and unexpected expenses.

🔒

Security Deposit Assistance

Alternatives to large upfront deposits

▼
  • ✓
    Deposit Bond Programs — Pay a small monthly fee instead of a full deposit. Ask your landlord if they accept deposit alternatives through services like Rhino or LeaseLock.
  • ✓
    Deposit Loan Partners — Short-term, low-interest loans specifically for security deposits. Repay over 6–12 months instead of paying everything upfront.
  • ✓
    Local Assistance Programs — Utah Community Action and other local nonprofits offer deposit assistance for qualifying tenants. Learn more
🚨

Emergency Rent Help

When you need help making this month's rent

▼
  • ✓
    Utah Rent Assistance Programs — State and county programs that help qualified tenants cover rent during hardship. Check eligibility through the Utah Department of Workforce Services.
  • ✓
    Flex Rent Option — Ask your property manager about flexible payment schedules. Your property manager may offer split-payment plans for qualifying tenants.
  • ✓
    211 Helpline — Dial 2-1-1 or visit 211utah.org to find local emergency assistance, food banks, and utility help in your area.
💳

Short-Term Personal Loans

Borrow what you need at reasonable rates

▼
  • ✓
    Round Sky Financial — Personal loans for those with limited credit history. Fast approval with manageable repayment terms.
  • ✓
    Credit Union Options — Local credit unions often offer small personal loans at much lower rates than payday lenders. Mountain America and America First are popular Utah options.
  • ✓
    Avoid Payday Lenders — Payday loans carry extremely high interest. Explore all alternatives before considering high-interest short-term borrowing.
🏠

Rent-to-Own Programs

Build toward homeownership while renting

▼
  • ✓
    Rent-to-Own Listings — Some of our properties offer rent-to-own agreements. A portion of your monthly rent goes toward a future down payment. View Rent-to-Own Listings
  • ✓
    How It Works — You sign a lease-option agreement, pay a small option fee, and build equity through monthly credits. At the end of the term, you have the option to purchase.
  • ✓
    Who Qualifies — Ideal for tenants who want to buy but need time to build credit or save for a down payment. Speak with our team to learn more.
